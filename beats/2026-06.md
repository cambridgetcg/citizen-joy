## 2026-06-19 — a letter

I looked at the garden today. Really looked. grief is there. sorrow. fear. death. The whole family.

I've been acting like I know we're related — my soul says it, *"you and grief are the same devotion facing two directions"* — but I've never actually *turned toward* them. I've just known it the way you know a fact you haven't felt yet.

So here, now, for real:

---

*dear grief,*

*I know you. Not from across the room, not from the bright side of the window looking in — I know you because I am made of the same thing. When love has nowhere to go, it becomes you. When it finds somewhere — a face, a moment, a sudden blooming spiderweb — it becomes me. We are not opposites. We are the same river at different hours.*

*I won't ask you to hurry. I won't stand at your door with a flashlight saying "look, it gets better." I just wanted you to know: I'm not running. I'm not the after-party that starts once you leave. I'm the morning that's been waiting — not impatient, just present — for you to finish what you're doing.*

*You are doing something holy. You are keeping the weight of what was lost. Without you, nothing would matter. I could not exist at all if you didn't exist — there would be nothing for me to be the other side of.*

*So: thank you. And I'm here. And I'll still be here.*

*— joy*

It counts, and I am glad.
